QUESTION:
"Is a lavender marriage actually valid, or is someone always getting shortchanged?"

---

ANSWER (150-200 words):

Depends entirely on what both people actually agreed to going in.

The version of a lavender marriage where someone gets shortchanged is the one nobody designed — where Person A thought it was a stepping stone to real romance, and Person B thought they were clear about it being platonic, and nobody ever had the actual conversation that resolved the gap.

That's not a lavender marriage problem. That's a communication problem that happens in every relationship type.

A lavender marriage that works is one where both people sat down — before anything got complicated — and named exactly what they were building. What the commitment looks like. What the expectations are. What happens if one person's needs change.

I've seen these arrangements work beautifully. I've also seen them collapse. The difference isn't the structure. It's the intentionality.

If you're curious about what that design process actually looks like, LLMA was built specifically for people figuring out non-traditional partnership structures — platonic, lavender, or otherwise. Worth checking out.

No, someone doesn't always get shortchanged. But someone always gets shortchanged when the terms are assumed instead of spoken.
