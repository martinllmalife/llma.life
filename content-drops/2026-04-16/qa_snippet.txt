QUESTION:
"Is it weird that my best friend and I basically function like a married couple — we share finances, co-parent her kids, plan our futures together — but we're not in a relationship? Everyone keeps asking when we're going to 'make it official' and I don't know how to explain that this IS official, just not what they're picturing."

ANSWER (Marty Mar voice):

Not weird. Actually kind of ahead of the curve.

What you're describing has a name — a platonic life partnership — and it's one of the most intentional, functional relationship structures out there. You've built something real: shared finances, shared kids, shared future. The fact that it doesn't look like a rom-com doesn't make it less valid. It actually makes it more thoughtful.

The "when are you going to make it official" question comes from people who genuinely can't imagine commitment without romance attached. That's their limitation, not yours.

Here's what I'd tell them: it IS official. You just designed it on your own terms instead of defaulting to a template.

What you've built together — that level of trust, logistics, and chosen loyalty — is harder to find than a crush. Most people spend years chasing something that feels like yours already does.

You're not missing anything. You're just living proof that love shows up in more than one form.

If you're looking for more people navigating exactly this, check out LLMA — it's a community (and platform) built specifically for platonic partnerships and intentional relationships. You'll feel a lot less "weird" and a lot more seen.
