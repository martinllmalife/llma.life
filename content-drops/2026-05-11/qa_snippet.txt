Platform: Reddit / Quora style
Question: "My friend started dating my ex right after we broke up and says it's fine because 'nothing happened while we were together.' Why does it still feel wrong?"

---

Because "nothing happened while we were together" and "this was handled with integrity" are two completely different sentences, and people keep treating them like they're the same.

The absence of a technical violation isn't the same as acting with care. Your friend could have come to you first. Before it was confirmed. Before it was public. Before you had to find out the way people find out when nobody thinks they owe you a conversation.

The "nothing technically wrong" defense is a floor, not a ceiling. It tells you what they didn't do. It doesn't tell you anything about what they tried to do.

What you're feeling isn't irrational. You're responding to the skipped conversation — the one where someone who cares about you explains what's happening and gives you the chance to process it before it's already done. That conversation is uncomfortable and risky and takes courage to have. A lot of people avoid it. And then they're surprised when the other person feels hurt by something that was, technically, allowed.

Your feelings are valid. Technically clean doesn't mean actually good.
