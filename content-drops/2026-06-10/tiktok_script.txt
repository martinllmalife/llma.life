And here's the thing nobody wants to say: time is not the variable.

You can spend 18 years building a real partnership — shared values, friendship, explicit commitments about what you are to each other beyond the romance — and when the romantic part changes, you still have all of that. The house is still standing. You just renovate one room.

OR you can spend one year, or five years, or eighteen years vibing into a relationship that was built entirely on feelings and forward momentum and the assumption that love would figure out the details. And when the romantic part shifts or someone betrays the trust? There's nothing holding the structure up. It collapses.

Jason and Jenny had a house. Megan and Klay had a vibe.

This is what LLMA has been saying forever. Romantic love is real. It's beautiful. It is also not a foundation. It's a room. And you need to intentionally design the rest of the house — the friendship, the commitments, the explicit values, the 'what are we actually building here' conversation — BEFORE you assume the feeling will handle it for you."
