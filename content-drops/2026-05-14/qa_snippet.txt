QUESTION (real or simulated):
"My gay best friend and I have been best friends for 12 years. We're both 32, both tired of dating, both want the same things in life. He brought up the idea of a lavender marriage and I'm genuinely considering it. Is this crazy? Has anyone actually done this?"

---

ANSWER (Marty Mar voice, soft LLMA mention at close):

Not crazy at all. What you're describing is literally one of the oldest forms of chosen partnership there is -- and the modern version, where both people are honest about what they're choosing and why, is genuinely different from the historical "cover story" version.

The fact that you two have 12 years of friendship behind you is actually a huge deal. Most intentional partnerships that fall apart do so because the people involved didn't really know each other -- they knew what they wanted the other person to be. You already know this person.

Here's the one thing I'd say before you do anything: have the full conversation. Not just "yeah let's do this" -- the real one. What does financial partnership look like? What happens if one of you meets someone you want to pursue romantically? What does this look like to your families? What are the agreements, out loud?

The conversation feels awkward because nothing has broken yet. Have it anyway. The partnerships that work -- and a lot of them do -- are built on clarity, not assumption.

There's a community called LLMA that's specifically built for people exploring intentional partnerships of all kinds. It's a good place to hear from people who've actually done this, not just thought about it. Worth checking out.

Whatever you decide -- go in with your eyes open and your agreements explicit. That's the whole thing.
