Question: "How do you know if your relationship is built on real partnership or just on 'looking good together'?"

---

A: This is actually one of the more useful questions you can ask yourself, and most people only ask it after something breaks.

The short version: a relationship built on looking good together will mostly be defined by external validators. How it photographs. How other people react to you as a unit. Whether the highlight reel holds up. It can feel deeply real from inside it, because external validation is genuinely satisfying. But when the validation stops — when you stop being aspirational, when the audience loses interest, when the circumstances change — there's nothing underneath holding it together.

A relationship built on real partnership has a different texture. It has agreements. Not necessarily formal ones, but actual conversations where you've landed on something. What does loyalty mean when things get complicated? What's the plan when money is tight? How do we handle it when one of us changes direction? What did we actually promise each other, in plain language, not in vows?

The way to know the difference isn't to look at how it performs. It's to look at how it holds up when there's no audience.

The people I've seen build the most durable partnerships — romantic, platonic, lavender, whatever the structure — all have one thing in common: they got bored talking about what the relationship looks like and got interested in what it actually needs.

If you're not sure which one you're in, that conversation is a good starting place.
