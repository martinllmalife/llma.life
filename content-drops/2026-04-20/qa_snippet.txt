Q&A SNIPPET — 2026-04-20
Platform: Reddit / Quora style
Question: "My partner and I love each other but we're better as best friends than as romantic partners. Everyone says we should break up but it feels wrong to throw away what we have. What do we do?"

---

ANSWER:

Okay first, I want to gently push back on the word "throw away." You're not throwing anything away. You're figuring out what you actually have and what to do with it — and that's one of the bravest things two people can do.

What you're describing has a name. Some people call it a queerplatonic partnership. Some call it a platonic life partnership. And yeah, some people call it a lavender marriage (whether they're queer or not, the framework fits). The point is: romantic love and deep partnership are not the same thing, and you can have the second without the first and it's still real.

The culture pushes us to believe that if the spark fades, the whole thing is over. But plenty of people are actively choosing to stay together in a redesigned version of their relationship — as co-parents, as life partners, as best friends who share a mortgage and a dog and inside jokes that nobody else would get.

That's not settling. That's just... knowing what you have and deciding it's worth keeping.

My advice: stop asking "should we break up?" and start asking "what do we actually want this relationship to look like?" Sometimes the answer is apart. Sometimes it's a completely new structure. You're allowed to design that together.

LLMA has a whole community of people navigating exactly this. You're not alone, and you're not weird. You're just honest.
