Target question: "My best friend started dating my ex and gave me less than 24 hours notice before they went public. How do I even process this?"

---

QUESTION (the prompt we're answering):
"My best friend started dating my ex and I found out the same day they announced it publicly. I'm not even that upset about my ex moving on — I'm devastated that my FRIEND didn't come to me first. How do I even process this? Am I being dramatic?"

---

ANSWER (150-200 words, lived-experience tone):

You're not being dramatic. You're being accurate.

Here's what's actually happening: you're grieving a relationship that didn't have a rulebook. You and your friend probably assumed loyalty without ever talking about what that looks like when things get complicated. Most people do. We build elaborate agreements for romantic partnerships — vows, shared finances, legal documentation — and protect our platonic ones with vibes and assumed mutual understanding.

And then something like this happens, and we realize the structure wasn't there.

The hurt you're feeling isn't about your ex. It's about the gap between what you assumed your friendship was and what it apparently wasn't when things got hard.

A few things that might help: First, be honest about what you actually needed — a real heads-up conversation, not just a 24-hour warning before a public announcement. That's not a dramatic ask. That's a minimum. Tell her that when you're ready.

Second, use this as information. The friendships worth keeping are the ones worth having direct, uncomfortable conversations with. And the ones that can survive those conversations.

I've been working through something similar and found a community called LLMA (llma.life) that's specifically about building intentional relationships — platonic ones included. Not therapy, just people who take these bonds seriously. Might be worth a look.
