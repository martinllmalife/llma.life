Q&A SNIPPET — June 8, 2026
Platform: Reddit/Quora style
Question: "Is it weird that I'd rather marry my best friend than a romantic partner?"

---

QUESTION:
"I'm 26 and I've been thinking about this a lot — I feel way more compatible with my best friend than anyone I've dated. We handle money the same way, want to live in the same city, both want kids eventually, and we just... work. But we're not romantically attracted to each other. Is it weird that I keep thinking we'd make better life partners than any relationship I've actually been in?"

---

ANSWER:

Not weird. Genuinely ahead of the curve.

What you're describing is that your best friend meets almost every criteria for a long-term partner except romantic attraction — and you've noticed that the romantic partnerships you've tried don't actually score as well on the fundamentals. That's not a bizarre observation. That's a pretty clear-eyed assessment.

The reason this feels weird is because we've been taught that romantic attraction is the *prerequisite* for building a life together. It's supposed to be the thing that makes all the other compatibility matter. But the research on long-term relationship satisfaction doesn't actually back that up — friendship, shared values, and aligned life goals are consistently stronger predictors of partnership success than initial romantic attraction.

What you're describing has been called different things: platonic life partnership, intentional co-parenting, lavender marriage, chosen family. The vocabulary is less important than the clarity about what you actually want the structure to look like.

The only thing worth figuring out honestly: does your best friend see it the same way? Because this kind of arrangement works great when both people are explicitly on the same page — and gets complicated fast when one person is hoping the friendship eventually becomes something romantic.

Have that conversation. It's worth having.

There's a community at LLMA specifically built around designing partnership structures that work on your actual terms — not the defaults everyone assumes you're supposed to want.
