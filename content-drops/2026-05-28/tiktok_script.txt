[BODY — 0:05-1:00]
Because here's the thing. Kyle wasn't suspicious because Amanda did something wrong. He was suspicious because she had an emotional connection with someone.

And in traditional romantic partnership? That's treated like a crime.

You're not allowed to have deep, meaningful emotional intimacy with anyone except your partner. Your feelings belong to them. Your close friendships get policed. Your DMs get audited. And the second someone outside the relationship knows you better than your partner does?

That's a "threat."

Think about how insane that actually is.

You meet one person. You fall in love. And now they're supposed to be your EVERYTHING. Your best friend. Your confidant. Your financial partner. Your roommate. Your family planner. Your emotional support animal. And also physically attracted to you.

Forever.

And if they're not all of that? If you need MORE? If you find emotional connection ELSEWHERE?

That's a red flag. That's suspicious. That's "I hear things."

Here's what nobody tells you: the problem isn't the emotional affair. The problem is the design. We're asking one person to be everything, and then acting shocked when emotional connections leak out everywhere.

Intentional partnerships -- lavender marriages, platonic life partners, co-primary relationships -- they don't pretend emotional connection is contained. They BUILD it in. Distributed, honest, agreed-upon.

Kyle and Amanda didn't fail because Amanda had feelings. They failed because they never agreed on what "having feelings" meant.
