SLIDE 2 — THE ACCUSATION
---
"Emotional affair" is just what romantic partnerships call:

Having a close friend.

Feeling understood by someone.

Being known outside your relationship.

That's not betrayal. That's being human.

---

SLIDE 3 — THE INSANE ASK
---
We pick ONE person.

And ask them to be:
- your best friend
- your therapist
- your financial partner
- your co-parent
- your roommate
- your hype person
- AND someone physically attracted to you

Forever.

No wonder emotional connection leaks out.

---

SLIDE 4 — THE REAL PROBLEM
---
The problem isn't the "emotional affair."

The problem is the design.

We built partnerships that can't handle human beings having multiple deep emotional connections.

Then act shocked when they do.

---

SLIDE 5 — THE ALTERNATIVE
---
Intentional partnerships do this differently.

They ask the hard questions FIRST:

"What emotional connection do I need that you can realistically provide?"

"What does closeness with other people look like in this partnership?"

"What are the actual agreements here?"

Not romantic. But sustainable.

---
Kyle and Amanda didn't fail because someone cheated.

They failed because nobody gave them the tools to design their emotional agreements.

Nobody gives anyone those tools.

LLMA is trying to change that.
