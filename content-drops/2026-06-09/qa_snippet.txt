Topic: Is it disrespectful to pursue someone from your friend group while dating someone else?

---

QUESTION (sourced from r/relationship_advice style):
"My close friend started pursuing me romantically while he was still seeing someone. He didn't tell me until I found out from someone else. Now he says the other relationship 'wasn't serious' but I'm not sure I trust that framing. Am I overreacting by feeling hurt?"

---

ANSWER:

You're not overreacting. What you're describing isn't about whether the other relationship was serious — it's about the fact that you were operating with incomplete information while he was making decisions about your friendship.

That's the actual thing. Not the other person, not the timing, not whether it counts as "official." He had information that was relevant to how you'd want to navigate this, and he chose not to share it.

The "it wasn't serious" reframe is very common and it usually does two things at once: it minimizes the other person's experience, and it sidesteps the real question, which is why didn't you just tell me?

In my experience, the friendships and partnerships that survive messy transitions like this one are the ones where someone said the uncomfortable thing out loud before the situation forced their hand. You can absolutely decide to give this person grace and move forward — people do it all the time. But I'd want him to name what actually happened first, not just reassure you that the other thing didn't count.

The honest conversation is always available. It just takes someone willing to have it before the Bravo reunion couch, so to speak.
